# NUS-SYNAPXE-IMDA AI Innovation Challenge 2026

## Problem Statement 2: AI for Multimodal Remote Health and Wellness Monitoring

**Objective:** Design an AI-powered system that uses visual data from cameras, webcams, or device cameras, possibly combined with other modalities such as audio, motion sensors, or wearable sensors, to monitor health or wellness indicators remotely. The solution should enable accessible, non-contact, and continuous monitoring of wellness, stress, physiological signals, posture, or activity beyond traditional clinical settings.

**Possible Directions:**
- Estimating physiological signals such as heart rate, respiration, fatigue, or stress from facial or upper body video
- Detecting emotional or cognitive states, stress, or mental wellness indicators through behavior or expression analysis
- Assessing movement quality, posture, physical activity, or mobility through pose estimation or motion tracking
- Combining video-based analysis with audio, motion, sensor, or contextual data for holistic wellness assessment
- Enabling remote or continuous wellness tracking for users at home, individuals with mobility challenges, elderly living alone, or in community settings

**Deliverables for this track:**
- A clear definition of the health or wellness metric or condition being monitored or predicted
- A justification of why multimodal AI is appropriate compared to conventional assessment methods
- A functional demonstration of the proposed approach. This may include prototype components, simulations, analytics pipelines, or conceptual workflows
- A data handling and privacy plan including consent, anonymization, or use of synthetic data
- A presentation showing how the system works, how users would be monitored safely, and how results are interpreted
- Considerations for ethics, user comfort, data security, and practical deployment feasibility

## Expected Deliverables for All Participants
Regardless of the problem statement chosen, each team is expected to submit:
- A concise problem statement and context analysis describing the healthcare or wellness gap in Singapore
- A rationale for adopting AI that explains why conventional approaches are insufficient and how AI adds value
- A functional demonstration or proof of concept illustrating core functionality. This may include prototype components, simulations, or model workflows
- A data strategy and ethics plan describing data handling, privacy, consent, and deployment considerations
- A presentation summarizing the solution, technical approach, user journey, expected benefits, potential challenges, and roadmap for adoption
- An evaluation plan or proposed metrics for measuring potential impact such as clinical outcomes, wellness indicators, user adoption, or scalability

## The National Multimodal Large Language Model (LLM) Programme (NMLP)
Launched in 2023 by IMDA, AI Singapore, and A*STAR. Funded at S$70 million to develop sovereign localized LLMs: **MERaLiON** and **SEA-LION**.

### Recommended Models

**1. MERaLiON – Multimodal Empathetic Reasoning and Learning in One Network**
- **Developed by:** A*STAR I²R
- **Focus:** Natural speech, paralinguistic cues (tone, pitch, emotion), empathetic interaction.
- **Key Features:** Natural speech understanding, code-switching support (Singlish, local dialects).
- **Use Cases:** Elderly care support (home buddy), Healthcare Rehabilitation (robot interaction).
- **Link:** https://www.meralion.ai/

**2. SEA-LION – Southeast Asian Languages in One Network**
- **Developed by:** AI Singapore
- **Focus:** Text and vision capabilities (v4) adapted for SEA cultures and 11 regional languages.
- **Key Features:** Translation, summarization, document processing with deep cultural grounding.
- **Use Cases:** Volunteer matching, migrant worker support.
- **Link:** https://sea-lion.ai/
